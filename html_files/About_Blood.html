About Blood

Whole blood consists of 3 types of blood cells, suspended in a liquid called plasma. Blood circulates through the arteries and veins with each of the blood cell types– red blood cells, white blood cells and platelets– carrying out different functions throughout the body.

  • Red Blood Cells (RBC) – Erythrocytes

    They are the most abundant type of cells in the blood. They are bowl-shaped and lack a nucleus, hence can easily squeeze through narrow vessels. Their many role in the body is to carry out Hemoglobin, which helps carry oxygen from the lungs to the rest of the body. RBCs appear red due to the presence of hemoglobin in them

  • White Blood Cells (WBC) – Leukocytes.

    They are much fewer in number and protect your body from infection and hence are an integral part of the body’s immune system. There are five different types of WBCs with very characteristic functions, namely: Neutrophil, Basophil, Eosinophil, Monocyte and Lymphocyte.

  • Platelets – Thrombocytes

    Unlike RBC and WBC, these are small fragments of cells. Platelets help the blood clotting process during any injury or a site of broken skin. They clot by gathering at the site of an injury, sticking to the lining of the injured blood vessel, and forming a platform on which blood coagulation can occur. This results in the formation of a fibrin clot, which covers the wound and prevents blood from leaking out. Fibrin also forms the initial scaffolding upon which new tissue forms, thus promoting healing

Linking personality and Blood Types

We have an interesting theory from the Japanese, linking personality traits with blood categories, this is called the Japanese Blood Type Personality Theory. This links personality trait with each blood group. When someone behaves in certain way, the Japanese advocates this behavior to the blood group. So before getting into relationship if you want to know if they are perfect match you can look into their blood groups.
